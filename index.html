About

Profile

I graduated in 2025 with a Master's degree in Animation from LUCA School of Arts in Genk. Since the beginning of my animation studies, I have developed a strong passion for the versatile 3D software, Blender. Over the past 6 years, I have used this program daily for both 3D and 2D animation (including modeling, rigging, texturing, and more), as well as sculpting, visual effects (VFX), and compositing. I’ve explored nearly every aspect of Blender, pushing my boundaries to the point where I can work creatively with many of its features and techniques without limitations.

I’m also constantly playing and creating video games. That is why I started a game development course. I have now refined my skills inside Unreal Engine and learned about all the different aspects of the game development process.

I'm currently looking for opportunities in game studios where I can contribute across both art and tech disciplines.

Project 01

Startegic turn-based fighter game

This is a local multiplayer, turn-based fighting game set in a medieval arena. I made this game for my final game development exam. Unlike traditional turn-based games, both players choose their strategic moves via card-based mechanics while time is frozen, and then execute those actions simultaneously from a top-down perspective. I made this game in Unreal Engine 5 and created all the 3D assets and character rigs using Blender.

Startegic turn-based fighter game

Project 02

Cozy retro-style 3D modelling game

This is a prototype of a game where you need to create 3D models using only your keyboard. Every keyboard press has a specific function that modifies the primitive shapes. You can also record key presses which means every 3D model you make can be encoded into a series of keypresses. When you play the recording back, it recreates your model in the exactly the same way.

3D Animated Short Film

The Burden

I made The Burden for my animation master thesis. It is a 3D animated short film about a greedy man in the desert who builds a settlement on the back of a giant tortoise. The story serves as an allegory for humanity’s indifference to its destructive impact on nature. Where the old man represents humanity and the tortoise symbolizes nature.

Synopsis

In a desolate, endless desert, a giant turtle struggles with a heavy burden on her back. The burden is a growing pile of metal waste, accumulated by an old, greedy man who lives on her shell. Using a magnet and a fishing rod, he scavenges for anything that seems valuable, while steering the turtle with a chain. Tensions between the man and the turtle rise as the heap of junk grows and the turtle becomes exhausted. Blinded by his obsession to collect more, the man ignores the signs of the animal’s deterioration. As they travel through a massive junkyard, the man seizes the opportunity to expand his settlement. The pile of waste on the turtle’s back quickly transforms into a towering structure. Eventually, the burden becomes too heavy for the turtle, and the tower collapses violently. The man is buried under the rubble, while the turtle retreats into her shell to protect herself. At sunset, the turtle emerges from her shell and continues on her own path.
